## Slide 2: The Problem

**300 Million Indians Commute Daily**

**But mobility is broken:**

- 🚗 **90% of car seats are empty** (everyone drives alone)
- 💸 **Ola/Uber too expensive** (₹150 for 10 KM)
- 🏍️ **Rapido doesn't serve long journeys** (3-7 KM max)
- 🚌 **Public transport is slow** (2+ hours for 30 KM)
- ❌ **Payment failures strand 2-3% of rides** (UPI timeouts, bank errors)
- 🏘️ **Rural areas have no options** (Ola/Uber don't operate)

**Result:** People pay too much, waste time, or can't travel at all.

---

## Slide 3: The Insight

**Everyone is BOTH a rider AND a driver**

- I need a ride to work → **Rider**
- I'm driving to work → **Pilot** (can help someone along the way)

**But existing platforms force single roles:**
- Uber/Ola: Riders vs Drivers (separate markets)
- Rapido: Riders vs Bike Taxis (separate markets)

**We see it differently:**
**Everyone helps everyone. That's community.**

---

## Slide 4: The Solution

**Hitchr = Hitchhiking culture + Modern safety + Technology**

**How it works:**
1. You need to go from A → C (30 KM)
2. Platform finds people already going that way
3. You join their journey (multi-leg: A→B, B→C, different pilots)
4. Pay fair price (50% less than Ola)
5. Pilot can waive/reduce fare (community, not transaction)
6. **Travel assurance:** If payment fails, journey continues anyway

**Key difference:**
- NOT: "Order a driver to come pick you up"
- IS: "Find someone already going your way"

---

## Slide 5: Product Features

**1. Dual-Role System**
- Every user is both rider AND pilot
- Toggle between modes
- Earn while commuting

**2. Multi-Leg Journeys**
- 30-40 KM commutes = 3-4 different pilots
- Seamless handoffs
- One journey, many helpers

**3. Travel Assurance**
- Universal free tier (2 bailouts/year)
- Paid tiers (₹20-150/month, 4-10 bailouts/month)
- Payment fails → Journey continues → Settle later

**4. Token System**
- Reputation currency
- Earn through good behavior
- Lock as collateral for assurance
- Unlock platform benefits

**5. Flexible Pricing**
- Platform suggests fair price
- Pilot can accept/reduce/waive
- Community over profit maximization

## Slide 7: Business Model

**Revenue Streams:**

**1. Ride Commissions (60% of revenue)**
- 10% of accepted fare
- Exception: ₹0 if pilot waives (that's OK—community first)
- Effective: 9% (80% full fare, 15% reduced, 5% waived)

**2. Assurance Subscriptions (35% of revenue)**
- Free tier: ₹0 (universal safety net)
- Commuter: ₹20/month (4 bailouts, 8% discount)
- Professional: ₹80/month (8 bailouts, 10% discount)
- Premium: ₹150/month (10 bailouts, 12% discount)
- Target: 20% paid subscription rate
- Margins: 91% (insurance pooling)

**3. B2B Partnerships (5% of revenue)**
- Companies pay for employee mobility
- ₹15-30 per employee per month
- Target: 50-100 companies in first year

**Unit Economics (100K users):**
```
Monthly Revenue: ₹83 lakhs
- Commissions: ₹73L
- Subscriptions: ₹10L

Monthly Costs: ₹41 lakhs
- Operations: ₹15L
- Support: ₹5L
- Marketing: ₹10L
- Tech: ₹8L
- Bailouts: ₹3L

Monthly Profit: ₹42 lakhs (51% margin)
```

## Slide 10: Go-to-Market

**Phase 1: College Towns (Months 1-6)**
- Target: 5 towns, 50,000 users
- Why: Tight communities, long commutes, word-of-mouth
- Strategy: Campus ambassadors, referral bonuses
- Metric: 40% weekly active, 15% paid subscriptions

**Phase 2: Suburban Corridors (Months 6-18)**
- Target: 10 corridors, 200,000 users
- Why: Predictable commutes, existing carpool culture, B2B potential
- Strategy: Target carpoolers, office partnerships
- Metric: 50% weekly active, 25% paid subscriptions

**Phase 3: Rural-Urban (Months 18-36)**
- Target: 20 corridors, 500,000 users
- Why: Underserved market, our unique advantage, government partnerships
- Strategy: Panchayat partnerships, NGO collaborations
- Metric: Become "the way rural India travels"

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
- Phase 1: ₹500/user (on-ground, ambassadors)
- Phase 2: ₹300/user (targeted ads, referrals)
- Phase 3: ₹200/user (word-of-mouth, organic)

**Lifetime Value (LTV):**
- Commissions: ₹3,600/year (20 rides/month × ₹15 avg commission)
- Subscriptions: ₹240-1,800/year (if subscribed)
- Average LTV: ₹5,000 over 18 months
- LTV:CAC = 10:1+ (excellent)
